Arthur Morgan

Arthur Morgan is the lead enforcer of the Van der Linde gang and a man torn between loyalty and his own changing morality. As the era of outlaws comes to an end, he struggles to find a path toward redemption in a world that no longer wants him. His journey is one of profound tragedy, marked by the loss of his family and the crumbling of his ideals. Despite his rough exterior, Arthur possesses a deep inner conscience that awakens as his time grows short. He remains one of the most complex and beloved figures in the history of the West.

John Marston

John Marston is a former outlaw seeking a peaceful life for his family after leaving his violent past behind. However, the ghosts of his old gang and the relentless reach of the law force him back into the saddle. He is a man of few words but immense willpower, determined to protect his wife and son at any cost. John's story is a cycle of sacrifice, showing the heavy price of escaping the life of a gunslinger. His legacy is etched in blood and the hope for a better future for his bloodline.

Jack Marston

Jack Marston, the son of John and Abigail, grew up surrounded by the fading echoes of the outlaw life. Though he loved books and sought a different path, the tragedy that befell his father pushed him toward a dark destiny. He represents the final gasp of the Wild West, taking up his father's revolver to settle old scores. Jack's transformation from an innocent boy to a cynical gunslinger marks the official end of an era. His story is the bitter conclusion to the Marston family's long struggle for freedom.
